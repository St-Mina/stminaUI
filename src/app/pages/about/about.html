Welcome to St. Mina
Coptic Orthodox Church

St. Mina Coptic Orthodox Church is a welcoming parish founded in the rich tradition of the Coptic Orthodox Church — one of the oldest Christian communities in the world, tracing its roots to St. Mark the Apostle in the first century.

Our church is named after the great Egyptian martyr and wonder worker St. Mina, whose intercessions have brought miracles to countless believers throughout the centuries.

We are a diverse community of families, young adults, and individuals united by our love for Christ and our commitment to preserving the ancient apostolic faith while serving our modern community.

Our History

Faith

Coptic Orthodox Tradition

The Coptic Orthodox Church was founded by St. Mark the Evangelist in Alexandria, Egypt, around 42 AD. It is one of the oldest Christian churches in the world, preserving the faith, liturgy, and monasticism of the early Church.

Patron

St. Mina the Wonder Worker

St. Mina was an Egyptian soldier and martyr who gave his life for Christ during the Roman persecution. He is beloved for the many miracles associated with his intercession, and his monastery near Alexandria remains a major pilgrimage site.

Today

A Growing Community

Our parish continues to grow, welcoming new families and individuals into our community. We are committed to serving our neighbors, educating our youth, and glorifying God in all we do.

What We Believe

The foundations of our Orthodox faith

The Holy Trinity

We believe in one God — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — three Persons in one divine essence.

The Incarnation

God the Word became incarnate through the Virgin Mary, fully God and fully man, to save humanity.

The Sacraments

We celebrate seven holy sacraments as means of grace, including Baptism, Chrismation, and the Eucharist.

The Saints

We honor the saints, especially the Theotokos (Virgin Mary), as intercessors and examples of holy living.
